What happens in Anambra Central if Soludo backs the LP candidate and APGA fields a weak alternative?
The model attributes Labour Party's 6.2-point lift in Anambra Central — from the 47.2% baseline to 53.4% under this scenario — to two reinforcing forces. First, a turnout rebound in the 18–34 cohort, which has been the constituency's most volatile slice across 2019 and 2023[1]. Second, a weakening of APGA's ground operation following the Soludo–Obi alignment signal, which historically carried 41% of his personal vote into the next vehicle he endorsed[2]. The model also notes that BVAS-failure risk in Onitsha North remains a tail factor; if more than 12% of polling units fail to record, the band widens by roughly 1.4 points[3].
