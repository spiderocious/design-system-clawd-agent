From Marcus's Sunday notes

"Why the second derivative isn't 'the slope of the slope'"

12 April 2026

Most students arrive at the second derivative thinking it is just the slope of the slope. That's true the way nutrition is just the sugar in the apple. It is technically correct and it explains nothing.

The second derivative tells you how a quantity's rate of change is itself changing — which is the same thing, said with a tense that makes it matter. A ball thrown straight up has a velocity that is shrinking, then negative, then growing more negative. The thing that doesn't change is the acceleration: minus 9.8. The second derivative is the calm thing under the noisy thing.

When you start asking "what is the calm thing under this?" across other problems, you have begun to think the way the curriculum quietly hopes you will.
