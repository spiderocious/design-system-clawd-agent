# Pracket · MVP Feature List

## Introduction

Pracket is a tutor-meets-student marketplace where parents and students find verified tutors, pay a one-time connection fee to open a conversation, and communicate entirely on-platform. Tutor identities are masked until both sides choose to share contact details outside the product. The platform earns on connections, not on lesson transactions.

## Summary

The MVP covers three actor groups — parents/students, tutors, and the platform trust team — across three core flows: discovery, connection, and conversation. Lesson payments happen off-platform; Pracket only collects the connection fee.

---

## Modules

### 1. Discovery

Users will be able to search for tutors by subject, academic level, location, lesson format (online or in-person), price range, and verification status when they land on the search page.

Users will be able to sort search results by relevance, price, or years of experience when they want to narrow down a long list.

Users will be able to read a tutor's full profile — including their bio, credential summary, lesson rate, connection fee, availability, and any Sunday notes they've published — when they click through from a search result.

Users will be able to save tutors to a shortlist when they want to compare options before committing to a connection.

---

### 2. Tutor Profiles

Tutors will be able to register an account and build a public profile — including a photo, bio, subjects taught, academic levels covered, lesson rate, preferred format, and availability windows — when they join the platform.

Tutors will be able to upload credential documents (degree certificates, government-issued ID, and references) for review when they submit their profile for verification.

Tutors will be able to write and publish Sunday notes — short blog-style articles visible on their profile — when they want to give prospective students a sense of how they think and teach.

Tutors will be able to update their availability, lesson rate, and profile visibility at any time from their account settings.

---

### 3. Connection

Users will be able to pay a one-time connection fee to open a conversation with a tutor when they decide they want to reach out.

Users will be able to review a full cost breakdown and confirm the non-refundable charge before completing the connection when they reach the payment step.

Users will be able to receive a receipt for the connection fee immediately after payment when the transaction completes.

Users will be able to request a refund when a tutor fails to reply to their first message within 24 hours of the connection being opened.

---

### 4. Conversation

Users will be able to send and receive messages with connected tutors entirely on-platform when a connection has been established.

Users will be able to see that any phone numbers, email addresses, or external handles they type into the chat are automatically redacted before the message is delivered when they attempt to share contact information.

Tutors will be able to receive and reply to messages from parents and students who have paid to connect with them when a connection is opened.

Tutors will be able to see a masked version of the parent or student's name (first name and last initial only) in all conversation views when they receive a message.

---

### 5. Trust & Safety

Tutors will be able to have their uploaded credential documents reviewed and verified by the Pracket trust team before their profile is listed publicly when they submit for verification.

Users will be able to see a clear verification status on every tutor profile and search result when they are browsing, so they can make an informed decision before connecting.

Users will be able to report a tutor and close a conversation when they encounter behaviour that violates platform rules.

The trust team will be able to review all incoming reports, take action on the reported tutor's account, and notify the reporting user of the outcome when a report is submitted.
