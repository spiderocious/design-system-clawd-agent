Buttons

three weights · one irreversible · the rest is restraint

A button system with three actual weights — primary, secondary, quiet — and one ornament: the irreversible button, used for actions that cannot be taken back. Everything else (split, toolbar, icon-only) is a layout of those three. No glow, no gradient, no scale-on-press.

Sign & submit

Primary action, end of a chart-open task. Lives at the bottom-right of the sheet.

The footer

Quiet on the left for "save draft," secondary for "back," primary for the action that ends the task. Never two primaries.

In a row

Quiet buttons sit alongside content. They never compete with the row's primary content.

The list-row

One quiet primary at the end. Secondary and below are kebab-revealed. Hover state on the row reveals the action — it is not always visible.

In a toolbar

Icon-only buttons on a single sheet. Tooltips carry the names; the icons carry the meaning.

The chart toolbar

Three groups separated by hairline rules: navigate, annotate, and write. The active tool is filled. Nothing more.

Split — primary with related

For an action that has near-twins. The arrow opens a small menu of co-authors of the same intent.

Sign & …

"Sign & submit" is the default. The chevron offers "Sign & addendum," "Sign with co-signer," "Sign & print AVS."

Irreversible

For actions that cannot be undone — discharge AMA, override an allergy, void a signed order. Held, not clicked.

Hold to confirm

Black ink with a hairline of arterial red beneath the label. On press, the red rises through the button over 1.5 seconds. Release before full and the action is cancelled.

confirmed actions log to the audit record with name, time, workstation
