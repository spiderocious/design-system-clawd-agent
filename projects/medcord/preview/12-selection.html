A drawn ink circle with an ink dot. Mutually exclusive choices, four at most before they become a select.

The dot

Same square-becomes-tick logic, but as a circle. Use for short lists; if it's longer than four, use a select.

Switches — the only "modern" idiom.

Tight, mono-grey when off, full ink when on. For binary preferences only — never for actions.

When to use

Settings panels: alarm tones, notifications, auto-page on critical, tabular figures. Never replaces a button.

Auto-page attending on critical lab
Sends a page if a value crosses the critical line.
Tabular numerals in tables
Aligns digits in lab and vital columns.
Alarm tone — bedside
Plays an audible tone on alarms armed at this workstation.

Disabled by hospital administrator.

Chips — filters & allergies.

Two uses: filter chips on a list (toggle on/off) and content chips inside a multi-value field.

Filters

The active filter is filled ink; others are outline. Use a chip when the filter is one-of-many; switch to a select when it is one-of-twenty.
