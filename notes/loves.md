# Reference products the user loves

Append-only. Adding a new entry here is one of the fastest ways to triangulate a stance for a new project — match the *feeling* of one of these.

---

## 2026-05-01 · Lumen Design System

The user pointed at a separate Lumen Design System as the bar. What it does well, in their reading:

- Warm-leaning neutrals with a slight olive cast (rejecting cool slate grey)
- A single confident accent (Horizon indigo) carrying every interactive state
- Density that feels purposeful, not just packed
- Domain components rendered as worked-out scenes with real content (the pay-run card, the workflow canvas with rotated diamond conditions, the inbox row)
- Quiet chrome — borders + a 1-px shadow, focus ring as a 3px tinted halo
- Tracking actually applied — display tight, body neutral, label slightly opened

**Use as reference for:** any system that needs to feel calm-confident, especially enterprise / B2B SaaS.

## 2026-05-01 · Medcord (this project's own surgical-paper rebuild)

The user's own approval bar. What works, in their reading:

- Bone-paper canvas with warm ink — material tension that reads as one substance, not two
- Apothecary green as the single accent, every other colour reserved for state
- The patient banner with the full-width allergy band in italic serif
- The bed board where empty / cleaning / off-line are typeset *differently* (struck-through, italic) instead of just coloured
- The EMR rendered as a binder — vertical tabs left, single-column running record middle, audit margin right
- Numbers shouting at 26-40px tabular monospace
- Buttons drawn in scenes (modal foot, list row, toolbar) instead of state grids

**Use as reference for:** the calibration bar. If your system isn't operating at this level of conviction, it is not finished.
