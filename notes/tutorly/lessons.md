# Pracket · session notes

## 2026-05-11 · variation pick is the load-bearing step
The user accepted the overall A stance (Oat & forest) immediately but stopped me mid-flow to swap the profile card to B and asked for **three alternative list-row designs (A1/A2/A3)** before committing. They picked A1 (lean line). Take-away: when offering A/B/C, the user reads each component on its own — they don't accept the package wholesale. Be ready to redo a single specimen with three new takes inside the same `_variations.html` rather than starting over. Continuation of the "scenes-over-catalogues" preference but at the variation-pick stage.

## 2026-05-11 · "let's lock in the design" means *go*, not *deliberate*
After A1 was picked, the user said "let's lock in the design now, what's next?" — they expected me to roll straight into Act IV with a plan, not another round of style questions. Take-away: once the variation is locked, summarise the build plan (foundation → primitives → scenes → overlays → register), confirm the two or three remaining choices (signature scenes, currency, etc.), then build without further check-ins until each major section completes.

## 2026-05-11 · multi-country product, single-currency design
The user wants the data model to be multi-country but the design surfaces to use Naira (₦) for now. Resolved by building a country-aware price specimen that demonstrates six currencies, while every scene uses ₦. Take-away: when a brief is "multi-X in code, single-X in design," the design carries one consistent locale and a single specimen page proves the component generalises. Don't sprinkle multi-currency through every surface — it reads as wishy-washy.

## 2026-05-11 · the "no gamification" veto reshapes the entire trust idiom
The user explicitly rejected gamification. That removed: star ratings, badges with checkmarks, streak flames, level-up indicators, and any medal iconography. The trust idiom became the `<span class="verified">Identity verified</span>` capsule (one shape, one phrase, repeated everywhere) plus credentials typeset as a printed CV. Take-away: when a marketplace explicitly excludes gamification, the trust system has to do work through **typography and verification, not iconography**. The verified capsule is a pattern worth pulling forward to other trust-required projects.
