# What worked

- **Going custom with a 26th stance.** None of the 25 catalogue stances fit "warm + sparse + B&W-with-semantic + coach voice." Inventing "Warm coach" let every discovery answer fall in place without compromise. **How to apply:** when the brief is consumer-friendly *and* B&W-disciplined *and* needs a coach voice, default to inventing rather than forcing a closest-match.
- **Warm canvas + cool accent.** The intentional tonal tension between `#FAF7F2` paper and `#533AFD` violet-blue accent is doing real visual work — the accent reads as a *signal*, not as decoration, because it's on a surface that doesn't share its hue family. Repeat: any time a B&W canvas needs a single accent, consider deliberately cool-on-warm or warm-on-cool.
- **Coach voice as a design constraint.** Adding "the AI speaks in second person" as a stance rule (not just a copy guideline) shaped every component — quote-style coach notes with violet left-rule, "the coach's note" as a UI primitive, the teleprompter live overlay. The voice IS the chrome.

# Decisions the user made mid-build

- **Accent shifted from moss-green to `#533AFD` after variation pick.** They picked variation A then asked for the violet-blue accent change. **How to apply:** in future variation picks, ask the accent question explicitly — it's the most likely thing to shift.
- **Full semantic palette (primary + amber + oxblood)** rather than single-accent or two-tone. The user wanted scoring states to read at a glance, which a single accent doesn't carry. **How to apply:** for any product that *displays* multi-dimensional scores or risk, default to offering the three-colour semantic option upfront.

# What to remember for next time

- **The user added an accent change mid-build, not at variation pick.** If a system can be re-skinned with one CSS variable swap (and ours could, because the accent is centralised), that's cheap. But it's a sign to ask the accent question harder in act III next time — not in the post-pick.
- **The current product had 7 dimensions of analysis** (sentiment / virality / brand / platform / timing / risk / tips). Building a real Analysis Result page that respects 7 tabs forced us to design the underline-tab system properly — the dimension-ring strip + the coach-notes panel + the inline-highlighted draft. That layout is reusable for any "AI-scores-N-dimensions" product.
- **Teleprompter was the clearest example of a justified "distinct" rule.** Same family, same accent, inverted canvas, oversized type. Performance contexts (recording, presenting, focus modes) usually deserve their own rules in the system — not their own design system.
